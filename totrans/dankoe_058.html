All Is Mind

Jimmy is a concept, created by you while reading this.

When you stop, Jimmy ceases to exist.

“I’m scared,” says Jimmy.

Poof — gone.

You may have heard spiritual gurus and philosophers mention that the universe is a giant mind.

How can that be?

Well, the mind makes sense of the world through concepts, stories, metaphors, and symbols.

To understand those concepts, we need to understand the network of concepts that make it a concept.

Why is your hand a hand? Is it still a hand without an arm? What about a cup? Is that cup meaningless without a hand to pick it up?

And gravity? What the fuck is gravity? Can you understand gravity without understanding force, gravitational constant, the mass of the object, and the distance between the centers of the masses? How do you determine the mass of an object? Can there be a “center” of a mass without there being an inner and outer?

Everything you know is just a layer of stories pieced together for your understanding.

If this email was written in Chinese, would it mean anything to you? No.

The mind makes sense and gives meaning to life through stories.

STORIES GIVE MEANING TO LIFE.

Your identity is the story you tell yourself. You will live out that story on autopilot if not made conscious. If not made conscious, it is impossible for it to end in your favor.

Are you the hero or the villain?

One cannot exist without the other.

The concept “hero” does not make sense without the concept “villain.”

These opposing concepts reflect the law of polarity. They create tension.

The concept of “life” does not make sense without “suffering.”

Now, whether these are labeled as “good” or “bad” is up to you.

Remember, it’s all a story. A game. Something within your control. Something within your mind.

Life implies suffering. Whether that suffering has a negative impact on you is determined by your level of consciousness and expectation.

If you expect life not to imply suffering, then you will suffer. Why? Because you are fighting against what is. You are trying to control the uncontrollable instead of flowing with it and controlling yourself within that flow.

If there is not a hero and villain of the story, it is boring. Meaningless. Unfulfilling. The story ceases to exist.

You are the actor. The player behind the character. The consciousness behind the mind.

Life is a dance between the hero and the villain.

Push pull. Wu wei. Yin yang.

The battle between good and evil is what propels you in any direction in life. If you are conscious of the battle, you move in a positive direction. If you are unconscious or resist the battle, you move in a negative direction.

The hero comes from humble beginnings. The hero levels up by playing the game. He fails… many times, but he doesn’t quit.

He get’s stronger. Cultivates a high value skill set. Rigs the game in his favor by taking full responsibility for the direction of his life.

After a few bouts with the villain, he prevails. His name is remembered. The impact he left on other’s is celebrated for centuries. His legacy is clear.
